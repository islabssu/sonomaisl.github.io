Get Involved

Whether you are a prospective student, a potential collaborator, or a supporter — there is a pathway for you at ISL.

Prospective Students

ISL welcomes undergraduate and graduate students interested in robotics, AI, sensing, and embedded systems. Research positions are available through directed study, capstone projects, and thesis supervision.

We look for students who are:

  • Self-motivated and comfortable working on open-ended problems
  • Interested in hands-on hardware and software integration
  • Willing to engage with the full stack — theory, implementation, and validation
  • Enrolled in Engineering, Computer Science, or a related program at SSU

To express interest, email Dr. Shrestha at shrestsu@sonoma.edu with a brief description of your background and what draws you to ISL's work.

Industry and Collaborators

We partner with industry and academic collaborators on applied research in intelligent systems, health sensing, and autonomous robotics. Current areas of active collaboration include:

  • Robotics and manipulation — sim-to-real, reinforcement learning, physical systems
  • Non-invasive biomedical sensing — breath VOC analysis, clinical pilots
  • Edge AI and embedded deployment — microcontroller inference, low-power sensing
  • Computer vision — classification, detection, language-guided systems

We are open to sponsored research, equipment partnerships, and co-advising arrangements. If you see alignment with your work, reach out.

Donors and Supporters

ISL operates on a combination of grants, university support, and philanthropic contributions. Equipment donations and targeted gifts directly support student research — enabling access to hardware, computing resources, and conference travel that would otherwise be out of reach.

The ISL Donation Fund is established through the Sonoma State University Foundation. Gifts of any size are welcome. For major gifts or equipment donations, please contact Dr. Shrestha directly.
